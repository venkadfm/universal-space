## What Grammarly Does Well

Grammarly is strongest at the final mile of writing. It catches grammar issues, improves clarity, flags tone problems and helps make emails or documents cleaner before sending.

In my workflow, I would use ChatGPT or Claude for early drafting, then Grammarly for final polish. That separation keeps the writing useful without letting one tool control everything. The last pass should still sound like you.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Is Grammarly worth it in 2026?

Grammarly is worth it if writing quality affects your work, grades, business emails or content output.

### Is Grammarly better than ChatGPT?

Grammarly is better for polishing existing writing. ChatGPT is better for ideation, outlines and full first drafts.

### Can Grammarly make writing sound robotic?

Yes, if you accept every suggestion. Use it to improve clarity while keeping your own tone.

### Who should use Grammarly?

Professionals, students, creators, job seekers and non-native English writers can all benefit from Grammarly.

### Is Grammarly enough for professional writing?

It is enough for grammar and clarity checks, but not enough for strategy, structure or expert judgment. Treat it as a final editor, not the writer.

### Should I use Grammarly with ChatGPT?

Yes. ChatGPT can help draft and brainstorm, while Grammarly can polish the final version inside your actual writing environment.
