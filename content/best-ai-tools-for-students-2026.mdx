## Tool-By-Tool Breakdown

### ChatGPT: Best For Understanding

Use ChatGPT to explain a concept, create examples, quiz you and show common mistakes. Do not use it to avoid reading. Use it to make reading easier.

### Perplexity: Best For Research

Use Perplexity when you need source-backed starting points. It is useful for essays, presentations and project research, but you still need to read and judge the sources.

### Notion AI: Best For Study Notes

Use Notion AI if your notes already live in Notion. It can summarize, reorganize and turn rough notes into revision material.

### Grammarly: Best For Final Writing

Use Grammarly after you have written your own draft. It is good for clarity and grammar, but it should not decide your argument.

### Canva Pro: Best For Presentations

Use Canva to make slides presentable quickly. The danger is spending too much time on design and not enough on the actual topic.

## How To Use AI Without Hurting Learning

Ask AI to explain concepts in three levels: beginner, exam answer and real-world example. Then close the tool and rewrite the answer in your own words.

This one habit makes AI useful without making you dependent on it. If you cannot explain the answer without the tool open, you have not learned it yet.

## Best Prompts For Students

| Goal | Prompt |
|---|---|
| Understand a topic | Explain this like I am new, then give an exam-style answer |
| Practice | Give me 10 questions, then check my answers |
| Revise | Turn these notes into flashcards and a summary |
| Research | Give me source-backed angles to explore for this topic |
| Essay planning | Help me create an outline, but do not write the essay for me |

## What Students Should Never Paste Into AI

- Exam login details
- Private student IDs
- Personal documents
- Unpublished research data
- Anything your school or university policy forbids
- A full assignment if the rules do not allow AI help

<ProTip title="Best prompt for students">
Explain this topic like I am preparing for an exam. Give me the core idea, one example, three common mistakes and five practice questions.
</ProTip>

## What To Avoid

Do not paste private student IDs, exam login details, unpublished research data or personal documents into random tools. Also avoid submitting AI-written work as your own if your school has rules against it. Apart from the policy issue, it weakens the one thing exams still test: whether you can think under pressure.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### What is the best free AI tool for students?

ChatGPT and Perplexity are good starting points because students can use them for explanations, practice questions and research discovery.

### Is Notion AI useful for students?

Yes, especially if you already organize notes in Notion. It can summarize, rewrite and turn rough notes into revision material.

### Can AI help with exam preparation?

Yes. AI is useful for flashcards, quizzes, explanations and practice questions, but it should not replace textbooks, classes or past papers.

### Which AI tool is best for presentations?

Canva Pro is useful for quick student presentations because it combines templates, visuals and simple editing.

### Can AI help with coding assignments?

Yes, but use it to understand errors and concepts. Do not submit code you cannot explain.

### Is AI allowed in school or college?

It depends on the institution and assignment. Always follow your school policy and ask the teacher if the rule is unclear.
